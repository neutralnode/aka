Why do you need a VPN in Nigeria?

In 2021, the Nigerian government banned Twitter for 7 months. MTN Nigeria, Airtel, Glo and 9Mobile all blocked access on their networks within hours of the government order. Millions of Nigerians bypassed the ban using VPNs — search trends spiked immediately after the announcement. The ban was eventually lifted, but the underlying legal framework that enabled it has not changed.

Beyond censorship, Nigerian ISPs — MTN, Glo, Airtel, and T2 (formerly 9Mobile) — are legally required to collect and retain user data. A VPN encrypts your traffic before it reaches the operator, so they cannot see what you are doing online. This matters especially when using public WiFi at Domino's, Cold Stone or any campus hotspot in Lagos or Abuja.
