Surfshark for Africa — detailed review

What works well: NoBorders mode activates automatically when Surfshark detects network restrictions — no manual configuration needed. During African internet shutdowns and social media blocks, this is the feature that keeps you connected. The unlimited device policy is a genuine differentiator in a market where households share connections and budget is a consideration.

African server locations: Surfshark has servers in Nigeria, Ghana and South Africa. This means lower latency for streaming African content (BBNaija, DStv, local services) and faster connections for users in West and Southern Africa compared to using a European server.

What could be better: No free plan. The cheapest option is $1.78/month on a 2-year plan — competitive globally, but still a real cost for many African users. PrivadoVPN is cheaper if price is the only criterion.
