Why these VPNs?

The freelance economy is one of the fastest-growing sectors in Africa — Lagos, Nairobi and Accra have large communities of developers, designers and writers working for international clients. A VPN is a professional tool for this work, not just a privacy option. It protects client data in transit, secures access to company systems, and unblocks platforms that are geo-restricted or throttled in some African countries.

Upwork has been blocked or restricted in some African countries. A VPN routes your traffic through a foreign server, making geo-restrictions irrelevant. For developers accessing client infrastructure over SSH or VPN tunnels, a personal VPN adds an additional layer of security on public WiFi in co-working spaces in Nairobi or Lagos.

Common questions
Do I need a VPN for Upwork in Africa?
In most countries, no — Upwork is accessible without a VPN. In countries with geo-restrictions, yes. A VPN also protects your client data when working on public WiFi.
Is a VPN safe for client work in Africa?
Yes — a VPN encrypts your connection, protecting client data in transit. Use Surfshark or ProtonVPN on public WiFi in co-working spaces.
Which VPN is best for freelancers in Africa?
Surfshark — unlimited devices, fast WireGuard protocol, and NoBorders mode if your country restricts freelance platforms.
