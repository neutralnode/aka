VPN for gaming in Africa — does it help?

The main issue for African gamers is latency — servers for popular games like PUBG Mobile, Free Fire, FIFA and Call of Duty are mostly in Europe or North America. A VPN can sometimes reduce ping by routing you through a faster path to the game server. But it can also increase ping if the VPN server adds distance.

The real use case for gaming VPN in Africa: accessing games released in other regions before local launch, bypassing geo-blocks on gaming stores, and protecting yourself from DDoS attacks during competitive play. Surfshark is the best option — its servers in South Africa, Nigeria and Ghana keep latency manageable for African users.

For gaming: always connect to the VPN server closest to your game server. For European game servers, use a European VPN server. For African game servers, use South Africa or Nigeria.

Which games are geo-blocked in Africa

Some game releases, DLC packs and in-app purchases are priced differently or unavailable in certain African countries. A VPN lets you access regional stores and purchase at local prices from other regions — this is legal in most countries and widely practiced globally.
