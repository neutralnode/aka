Why do you need a VPN in Equatorial Guinea?

Equatorial Guinea imposed a targeted shutdown on Annobon island on July 20, 2024 — cutting all mobile and internet services following protests against a Moroccan construction company's blasting operations. As of February 2026, this shutdown has been running for over 570 days, making it one of the longest active internet shutdowns anywhere in the world. Banking services, hospital communications and contact with the outside world were all severed.

On mainland Equatorial Guinea, internet censorship is selective but present. The government monitors online expression and has used targeted disruptions during political events. A VPN protects your privacy and provides access to blocked content.
