Why do you need a VPN in South Africa?

South Africa has no government internet censorship — it is one of the freest internet environments on the continent. But that does not mean a VPN is useless. South Africa has the highest cybercrime rate in Africa, and public WiFi networks at malls, coffee shops and co-working spaces in Johannesburg and Cape Town are frequently used as attack vectors. A VPN encrypts your connection end-to-end so no one on the same network can intercept your data.

For streaming, a South African VPN server lets you access DStv, Showmax and SuperSport from abroad, and lets you access international libraries not available locally. South Africans travelling to other African countries — where content is often geo-blocked — rely on VPNs to maintain access to their usual services.
