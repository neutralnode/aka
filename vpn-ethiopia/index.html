Why do you need a VPN in Ethiopia?

Ethiopia has imposed more internet shutdowns than almost any other country in the world. The Tigray region was cut off for over 987 days. The Amhara region was disconnected for nearly a year in 2023-2024. In February 2023, the government blocked Facebook, Telegram, TikTok and YouTube for 158 days following tensions with the Ethiopian Orthodox Church. During that period, VPN use exploded across the country.

The situation is unique compared to other African countries: Ethio Telecom has a near-monopoly on internet access in Ethiopia, with Safaricom only recently entering the market. When the state orders a shutdown, there is effectively one switch to flip. A VPN with strong obfuscation — like Surfshark NoBorders or ProtonVPN Stealth — is the only reliable way to maintain access.
