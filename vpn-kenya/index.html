Why use a VPN in Kenya?

Kenya blocked or throttled access to social media during the June-July 2023 anti-government protests following the finance bill controversy. Safaricom, which dominates Kenya's mobile market, complied with government directives. A VPN with strong obfuscation bypasses these restrictions when they occur.

The bigger daily use case is M-Pesa. Kenya runs more mobile money transactions than almost any other country — and most Kenyans access M-Pesa from their phones, often on public WiFi. A VPN encrypts the connection so no one can intercept your session. This applies equally to mobile banking apps like KCB, Equity or Co-op Bank.
