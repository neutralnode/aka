ProtonVPN for Africa — detailed review

The free plan: The most important thing to understand about ProtonVPN free is that it has no data limit — unique in the free VPN market. You can use it every day without running out. The trade-off is that free servers are in the US, Japan, Romania, Poland and Netherlands — meaning higher latency for African users. For everyday use this is manageable. For streaming, the paid plan with African servers is better.

Stealth mode: ProtonVPN's Stealth protocol wraps WireGuard in TLS on port 443 — the same port used by all HTTPS traffic. This makes it nearly impossible to block without breaking the entire internet, which is why it works in Egypt, Ethiopia and Uganda where other VPNs fail. The APK is available on protonvpn.com — critical when app stores are blocked during shutdowns.

What could be better: The paid plan at $3.99/month is more expensive than Surfshark or PrivadoVPN. For users who only need the free plan, this is irrelevant. For users who want African servers, the paid plan is worth considering — but Surfshark has more African locations.

Common questions
Is ProtonVPN really free with no limit?
Yes. ProtonVPN free has no data limit, no ads and no time limit. Servers are outside Africa on the free plan.
Does ProtonVPN work in Egypt?
Yes. ProtonVPN Stealth mode is specifically designed to bypass DPI systems like those used in Egypt. It is one of the most reliable options in high-censorship environments.
Where can I download ProtonVPN if the Play Store is blocked?
The APK is available directly on protonvpn.com — the official file, signed by Proton. Enable Unknown Sources in Android settings and install it directly.
